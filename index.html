Italy & Dolomites

July 2026  ·  ~12 days

A road trip through northern Italy — Rome for the first few days to shake off the jet lag and get into the rhythm, then north through Bologna and Venice before pushing into the Dolomites for the back half. The goal is to move slowly, eat well, and drive the kind of roads that make you pull over.

Cortina d'Ampezzo
Into the Dolomites. The drive itself is the event — SS51 through Cadore is spectacular.
⌄ Route detail
Venice (Mestre) → Cortina d'Ampezzo  ·  ~2h 30m  ·  A27 + SS51
Take the A27 north from Mestre to Belluno, then the SS51 up through the Piave valley. The last hour on the SS51 is the best driving of the trip — narrow valley, river on one side, Dolomite walls appearing ahead.
Stop in Belluno for espresso — pretty alpine town, worth 20 min
Lago di Centro Cadore viewpoint ~30min before Cortina — don't skip it
Fill up the tank in Belluno — Cortina fuel is expensive

Bolzano / Alta Badia
The Dolomite high road. Passo Falzarego, Val Badia, Alta Badia. Stop as much as you want.
⌄ Route detail
Cortina → Bolzano  ·  ~2h driving (budget 4–5h with stops)  ·  SS48 Dolomite Road
This is the best drive of the trip. The SS48 is the Great Dolomites Road — takes you over Passo Falzarego (2,105m), through Val Badia and Alta Badia. Do not rush this. The views from the Falzarego pass are as good as it gets in the Alps.
Passo Falzarego summit — pull over, walk 10 min toward the Lagazuoi cable car viewpoint
Alta Badia (La Villa / Corvara) — lunch at a rifugio if timing works out
Alpe di Siusi (Seiser Alm) detour adds ~45min but is the largest alpine meadow in Europe — worth it if you have energy
