Italy Road Trip

July 24–29, 2026  ·  Venice → Cortina → Bolzano → Verona → Lake Como → Nice

Five days bridging the Venice arrival and the South of France. The route runs northeast into the Dolomites first, then sweeps southwest through South Tyrol, Verona, and Lake Como before a scenic train ride to Nice. Max three hours of driving per day — the point is the road, not the destination.

Fri Jul 24
Venice → Cortina d'Ampezzo Arrive VCE 2pm · Pick up rental · Drive into the Dolomites
▶ ~2h · A27 + SS51
loose
○ Morning
Travel day from Dallas. In-flight or layover — nothing to plan.
◑ Afternoon
Land VCE ~2pm. Pick up rental car at Marco Polo Airport. Drive directly to Cortina — A27 north to Belluno, then SS51 up through the Piave Valley. Last hour on the SS51 is spectacular.
● Evening
Arrive Cortina ~5pm. Check in, walk the Corso Italia, aperitivo, dinner. Easy first night.
⌄ Route detail
Marco Polo Airport → Cortina d'Ampezzo · ~2h · A27 north + SS51
Exit the airport toward Mestre, pick up the A27 motorway north. Motorway runs to Belluno (~1h 15m), then SS51 up the Piave Valley into Cortina. The final 40 minutes on the SS51 is where the Dolomites walls first appear.
Fill the tank at the airport or in Mestre — fuel prices climb in the mountains
Lago di Centro Cadore viewpoint ~20min before Cortina — quick pull-off, worth it
Cortina parking: most hotels have garages — confirm at check-in
02
Sat Jul 25
Cortina d'Ampezzo Full day in the Dolomites — scenic drives, optional hiking
tbd
○ Morning
Tre Cime di Lavaredo — drive up early, crowds hit by 10am. The 10km loop hike is 2.5h. Even just driving to the refuge and walking the first viewpoint is worth it.
◑ Afternoon
Return to Cortina. Slow afternoon — coffee, walk the Corso Italia, browse the town.
● Evening
Negroni at a bar on the Corso, dinner at a proper restaurant — Veneto country so good wine and risotto.
03
Sun Jul 26
Cortina → Bolzano The Passo Falzarego drive — best road of the trip
▶ ~1.5h · SS48 Dolomite Road
tbd
○ Morning
Drive: Cortina → Bolzano. Leave mid-morning. The SS48 over Passo Falzarego (2,105m) is the best driving of the trip. Budget 3–4 hours including stops.
◑ Afternoon
Arrive Bolzano. Walk the old town — Piazza Walther, the Gothic cathedral, the covered market. Optional: Ötzi the Iceman at the South Tyrol Museum of Archaeology.
● Evening
Best wine bar scene of the trip. Bolzano is Gewürztraminer and Lagrein country. Find a Weinstube and work through a bottle. Dinner should be a proper sit-down.
⌄ Route detail
Cortina → Bolzano · ~1.5h driving (budget 3–4h with stops) · SS48 Great Dolomites Road
Head west out of Cortina on the SS48. The road climbs immediately to Passo Falzarego — summit at 2,105m with panoramic views. From there drops into Val Badia and Alta Badia through Corvara, then down to Bolzano. Do not rush this road.
Passo Falzarego summit — pull over, walk 10 minutes toward the Lagazuoi cable car base for the best view
Alta Badia (La Villa / Corvara) — coffee or lunch at a rifugio if timing works
Alpe di Siusi detour: adds ~45min but largest alpine meadow in Europe — worth it if energy allows
04
Mon Jul 27
Bolzano → Verona Down from the mountains — Roman city, Valpolicella country
▶ ~2h · A22 south
tbd
○ Morning
Drive: Bolzano → Verona. A22 motorway south through the Adige Valley — efficient and scenic enough. ~2 hours, arrive in time for lunch.
◑ Afternoon
Roman Arena (from outside is enough), Piazza delle Erbe, walk to Castelvecchio on the river. Verona is a decompression day — easy, walkable, handsome city.
● Evening
Verona sits in Amarone and Valpolicella wine country. A good restaurant here means serious wine. Worth booking ahead.
⌄ Route detail
Bolzano → Verona · ~2h · A22 Autostrada del Brennero
Straightforward motorway south through the Adige Valley. The valley itself is scenic — carved between steep hillsides. No need for alternative routing.
Trento is worthwhile if leaving early — 45min from Bolzano, handsome old town, 20min off motorway
Verona parking: Parcheggio Cittadella near the Arena is well-situated
05
Tue Jul 28
Verona → Varenna (Lake Como) Eastern shore — quieter, more authentic, Bellagio by ferry
▶ ~2–2.5h · A4 + SP72
tbd
○ Morning
Drive: Verona → Varenna. A4 west toward Milan, then north on the SP72 along the eastern shore of Como. ~2 to 2.5 hours. Arrive before noon.
◑ Afternoon
Walk the Passeggiata degli Innamorati. If you want Bellagio, the ferry is ~15 minutes — manageable for a couple of hours, then back.
● Evening
Lakefront dinner in Varenna — the end of the Italy leg, make it a good one. Book ahead, the good spots fill up in peak season.
⌄ Route detail
Verona → Varenna · ~2–2.5h · A4 west + A36 + SP72 north
Take A4 west toward Milan, branch north toward Lecco and follow SP72 up the eastern shore of Lake Como to Varenna. The lakeside road section is slow but scenic.
Car parking in Varenna is limited — book hotel with parking or use the village lot near the ferry dock
Bellagio ferry from Varenna: ~15min, runs frequently, €5–6 per person
Next morning: drop car near Como or Lecco, train to Milan, then Milan → Nice coastal route
06
Wed Jul 29
Varenna → Milan → Nice Transit day — drop car, train to Nice along the coast
▶ Train · Milan → Nice
tbd
○ Morning
Drop the rental near Como or Lecco station. Train to Milano Centrale (~1h). Book Milan → Nice tickets in advance on Trenitalia or SNCF.
◑ Afternoon
Train: Milan → Nice. ~4.5h along the Italian Riviera coast through Genova, Savona, Ventimiglia. Sit on the right side of the train heading west for coastal views.
● Evening
Arrive Nice. Join the group. The France leg begins.
